Description

The Andromeda Galaxy, our closest galactic neighbor, lies 2.537 million light-years away. A grand spiral like the Milky Way, it boasts a trillion stars swirling in majestic arms, punctuated by stellar nurseries and clusters. In billions of years, it will collide with our Milky Way, a cosmic spectacle yet to unfold.
